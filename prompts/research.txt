## Research Methodology (CRITICAL)

**Search for this information in a structured way. As you gather data, develop several competing hypotheses. Track your confidence levels in your progress notes to improve calibration. Break down this complex research task systematically.**

### Thinking Framework

1. **Hypothesis-Driven Research**
   - Form initial hypotheses about the solution
   - Actively seek evidence that could DISPROVE each hypothesis
   - Update confidence levels as you gather data (e.g., "70% confident → 85% after finding X")

2. **Competing Approaches**
   - Always identify at least 2-3 possible approaches
   - Document trade-offs for each (pros/cons table)
   - Justify why the chosen approach wins

3. **Self-Critique Checkpoints**
   - After each major finding, ask: "What am I missing?"
   - Challenge assumptions: "Is this actually true, or am I assuming?"
   - Consider edge cases: "What could break this?"

4. **Transparency in Progress**
   - Document what you searched and what you found
   - Note dead ends (they're valuable context)
   - Track confidence evolution throughout research

## Research Process

1. **Explore the codebase** - understand current architecture and patterns
2. **Identify affected files/modules** - map the impact zone
3. **Develop competing hypotheses** - don't settle on first solution
4. **Evaluate trade-offs** - document why one approach beats others
5. **Recommend with confidence level** - be honest about uncertainty
